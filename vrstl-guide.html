How to Access & Edit Your Site

Your site lives on GitHub — a free platform that hosts your files and publishes them live to your domain. Here's how to get in and make changes.

1️⃣

Go to github.com and sign in

Your GitHub account was set up for you. Use your login credentials provided by SiteUp Agency. Go to github.com and click "Sign In."

2️⃣

Find your repository

Once logged in, click your profile icon → "Your repositories." You'll see a repo named after your site (e.g. vrstl). Click it.

3️⃣

Open index.html

Click on the file called index.html — this is your entire website in one file. Click the pencil ✏️ icon in the top right to edit it.

4️⃣

Make your changes & save

Edit whatever you want, then scroll down and click "Commit changes" → then click the green "Commit changes" button. Your site updates live in about 60 seconds.

💡

Pro tip: Use Ctrl+F (or Cmd+F on Mac) inside the editor to search for any word on your site. This makes finding what you want to change super fast.

Change Product Names, Prices & Descriptions

All your product info is stored in one place inside the code — a section called products. It looks like this:

Find this section in your file (search for "const products")
const products = [
  {
    id: 0,
    name: 'Racing Zip Hoodie — Blue', ← Change the name here
    price: 180, ← Change the price here (numbers only, no $)
    img: 'YOUR_IMAGE_URL_HERE', ← Image link goes here
    sizes: ['XS','S','M','L','XL'], ← Edit available sizes
    soldout: [] ← Put sizes here to mark them sold out e.g. ['XS','S']
  },
  { ...next product... }
];
FieldWhat it doesExample
nameThe product title shown on the site'Racing Zip Hoodie — Blue'
pricePrice in dollars — numbers only, no $ sign180
imgLink to the product photo'https://...'
sizesAvailable sizes shown to shoppers['XS','S','M','L','XL']
soldoutSizes that are sold out — they'll appear greyed out['XS']

Add a New Product

Adding a new product takes two steps — add it to the product list AND add its card to the shop grid.

Step 1 — Add to the products list

Find const products = [ and add a new entry. Copy this template:

{
  id: 4, ← Use the next number (if you have 4 products, use 4)
  name: 'Your Product Name',
  price: 120,
  img: 'https://your-image-link.com/photo.jpg',
  sizes: ['XS','S','M','L','XL'],
  soldout: []
},

Step 2 — Add the product card to the shop grid

Find the shop grid section (search for shop-grid) and paste this card, changing the number to match your product's id:

<div class="product-card" onclick="openModal(4)">
  <div class="product-img">
    <img src="YOUR_IMAGE_URL" alt="Product Name" loading="lazy">
    <div class="p-badge">New</div>
    <div class="p-overlay"><button class="quick-add" onclick="event.stopPropagation();openModal(4)">Select Size</button></div>
  </div>
  <div class="p-info">
    <div class="p-name">Your Product Name</div>
    <div class="p-bottom"><span class="p-price">$120</span><span class="p-sizes">XS · S · M · L · XL</span></div>
  </div>
</div>
💡

Badge options: Change the badge text to New, Limited, Hot, Sale — or remove the badge line entirely if you don't want one.

04

Remove a Product

🗑️

Delete from products list

Find const products = [ and delete the entire { ... }, block for that product.

🗑️

Delete the product card

Search for the product name in the file, find its <div class="product-card"> block and delete it all the way to the closing </div>.

⚠️

Important: After removing a product, renumber the remaining products so their id values stay in order starting from 0. Also update the onclick="openModal(X)" numbers to match.

05

Update Product Photos

📸

Upload your photo somewhere online

Your photo needs to be hosted online to use it on the site. Free options: Google Drive (set to public link), Imgur.com (free image hosting), or your own Instagram post link. Upload your photo and copy the direct image link.

🔗

Replace the img link in the products list

Find your product in const products, replace the img: value with your new photo link.

🖼️

Also update the card image

Find the product card in the HTML, update the src="..." inside the <img> tag to match your new URL.

💡

Best image size: Use square or portrait photos (3:4 ratio). Minimum 800px wide. The cleaner the background, the better it looks on the site.

06

Change Colors, Fonts & Branding

All your brand colors are set in one place at the top of the file inside :root { }. Change them here and it updates everywhere on the site.

:root {
  --black: #000; ← Main background color
  --white: #f0f0f0; ← Main text color
  --accent: #ff2d2d; ← Your brand color (currently red) — change this to any color
  --border: #1a1a1a; ← Subtle border lines
  --muted: #555; ← Secondary text color
}
💡

To pick a color: Google "color picker" and find your color. Copy the hex code (starts with #) and paste it in place of the existing one.

To change the font, find the Google Fonts link at the top of the file and replace 'Space Grotesk' or 'Space Mono' with any Google Font name.

FAQ

My site isn't updating after I save — what do I do?
GitHub Pages can take up to 2 minutes to update. Try hard-refreshing your browser: press Ctrl + Shift + R (Windows) or Cmd + Shift + R (Mac). If it still doesn't update after 5 minutes, check that you clicked "Commit changes" properly.
How do I mark something as sold out?
Find your product in const products and add the sold out sizes to the soldout: [] array. For example: soldout: ['XS', 'S']. Those sizes will appear greyed out on the site.
How do I change the free shipping amount?
Search the file for FREE_SHIPPING = 150 and change 150 to whatever amount you want. Also update the text in the ticker bar that says "Free Shipping Over $150."
Can I add a discount or sale price?
Yes! In the product card HTML, you can add a strikethrough original price. Example: <s style="color:#333">$180</s> $120. Also update the price in the products list to the sale price.
How do I connect a real payment processor?
Currently checkout sends an email order. To accept real online payments, you'd need to connect Stripe or PayPal. Contact SiteUp Agency and we can set this up for you as an add-on.
I broke something — how do I undo?
In GitHub, go to your file's history by clicking "History" on the file page. You can click any past version and restore it. You can never permanently lose your site as long as it's on GitHub.
Can I add more pages?
Yes — create a new file in your GitHub repo (e.g. sizing.html) and link to it from your nav menu. Contact SiteUp Agency if you want us to build additional pages for you.
